Aspect Bosons Fermions
Spin Particles with integer spin (\(0, 1, 2, \ldots\)) Particles with half-integer spin (\(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{2}, \ldots\))
Quantum States The total wavefunction is symmetric under particle exchange: \[ \phi(r_1, r_2) = +\phi(r_2, r_1)\]. The total wavefunction is antisymmetric under particle exchange: \[ \phi(r_1, r_2) = -\phi(r_2, r_1)\].
Occupation Multiple identical bosons may occupy the same single-particle quantum state without restriction. Pauli exclusion principle applies: at most one fermion may occupy a given single-particle quantum state.
Examples Photons (quanta of light), phonons (lattice vibrations), plasmons (collective electron oscillations). Conduction electrons in metals at low temperatures, liquid \(^3\)He, and degenerate fermion systems such as neutron stars and white dwarfs.
