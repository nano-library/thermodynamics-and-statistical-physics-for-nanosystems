At a temperature \(T\), a particle has thermal energy \(E \propto kT\). Its momentum and de Broglie wavelength are determined by \(T\) and its mass \(m\). The transition from classical to quantum behaviour depends on the ratio between this wavelength and the average interparticle separation \(L\).
✦ Thermal energy
✦
\(E\) \(\propto\) \(kT\)
✦ Particle momentum
✦
\(P\) \(=\) \(\sqrt{2mkT}\)
✦ de Broglie wavelength
✦
\(\lambda_{\text{dB}}\) \(=\) \(h\) \(\sqrt{2mkT}\)
✦ Number density
✦
\(n\) \(=\) \(N\) \(V\)
✦ Interparticle separation
✦
\(L\) \(\propto\) \(n^{-1/3}\)
🌸
Classical regime

When \(\lambda_{\text{dB}} \ll L\), Classical statistics (canonical ensemble, partition function).

🌸
Quantum regime

When \(\lambda_{\text{dB}} \gg L\), Quantum statistics (grand canonical ensemble, Bose-Einstein or Fermi-Dirac).
