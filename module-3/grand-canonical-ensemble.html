✨ The grand canonical ensemble describes an open system in thermal and chemical equilibrium with a reservoir, allowing exchange of both energy and particles.
Let \(E\), \(E'\) and \(E^*\) be the energies of systems \(A\), \(A'\) and the total system \(A^*\), respectively.
Let \(N\), \(N'\) and \(N^*\) be the number of particles in systems \(A\), \(A'\) and \(A^*\), respectively.
Let \(E_r\) and \(N_r\) be the energy and number of particles of the \(r^{\text{th}}\) microstate of system \(A\).
Energy of the reservoir
✦
\(E'\) \(=\) \(E^* - E_r\)
Particle number of the reservoir
✦
\(N'\) \(=\) \(N^* - N_r\)
The probability of finding system \(A\) in a state with energy \(E_r\) and particle number \(N_r\) is proportional to the number of microstates of the reservoir:
Probability proportional to reservoir multiplicity
✦
\(P_r\) \(\propto\) \(\Omega'(E^* - E_r,\, N^* - N_r)\)
Expanding the logarithm of \(\Omega'\) to first order and using the definitions \(\beta = 1/(kT)\) and \(\alpha = -\mu/(kT)\), we obtain:
Exponential form
✦
\(P_r\) \(\propto\) \(e^{-\beta E_r - \alpha N_r}\)
where
\(\beta = \dfrac{1}{kT}\) , inverse temperature
\(\alpha = -\dfrac{\mu}{kT}\) , chemical potential scaled
Alternative form
✦
\(P_r\) \(\propto\) \(e^{-\beta E_r - \mu\beta N_r}\)
Alternative form
✦
\(P_r\) \(=\) \(e^{-\beta(E_r - \mu N_r)}\)
The grand canonical partition function is the normalization factor, summing over all possible states with different energies and particle numbers:
Grand partition function
✦
\(\mathcal{Z}\) \(=\) \(\displaystyle\prod_r \sum_{n_i} e^{-\beta(E_r - \mu N_r)}\)
For fermions, the occupation numbers \(n_i\) are restricted to 0 or 1:
Fermions
✦
\(\mathcal{Z}\) \(=\) \(\displaystyle\prod_i \left(1 + e^{-\beta(E_i - \mu)}\right)\)
For bosons, occupation numbers can be any non‑negative integer, leading to a geometric series:
Bosons
✦
\(\mathcal{Z}\) \(=\) \(\displaystyle\prod_i \frac{1}{1 - e^{-\beta(E_i - \mu)}}\)
