✨ In thermodynamics, every system obeys four fundamental laws; governing the existence of temperature, the conservation of energy, the rise of entropy, and the unattainability of absolute zero.
2.1

Laws of Thermodynamics

🌸
Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics
f A, B and C are different thermodynamical systems and A is in thermodynamical equilibrium with B and B is in thermodynamical equilibrium with C, then A is in thermodynamical equilibrium with C.
🌸
First Law of Thermodynamics
The internal energy of an isolated system is conserved under any thermodynamical change.
✦ Under any thermodynamical change,
\(\Delta\) U = Q - W
✦ Under an infinitesimally small thermodynamical change,
d U = \(\delta\) U + \(\delta\) W
🌸
Second Law of Thermodynamics
Consider an ideal gas expanding into a vacuum inside a thermally insulated container. Since no work is done and no heat is exchanged:
W = 0 , U = 0 \(\implies\) \(\Delta\) U = 0

The First Law permits the reverse process, yet it never occurs spontaneously. The Second Law governs this directionality, it determines the natural arrow of spontaneous change

Clausius Statement
Heat never flows spontaneously from a colder body to a hotter body.
Kelvin-Planck Statement
It is impossible to construct a cyclic device whose only net effect is to extract heat from a single reservoir and convert it completely into work.
Entropy Statement
For an isolated system, with equality for a reversible process and strict inequality for an irreversible process.
✦ Entropy
\(\Delta\) S \(\ge\) 0
🌸
Third Law of Thermodynamics
As the temperature approaches absolute zero, the entropy approaches a constant minimum value.
