When two macroscopic systems are brought into contact, they can exchange energy in the form of heat, work, or matter. These interactions define the fundamental processes that thermodynamics and statistical physics seek to describe and quantify.

Example: Two isolated systems brought into contact

Consider two systems \(A\) and \(A'\) initially isolated from each other, with no energy exchange. Let \(A^*\) denote the total system, with \((A, E, \Omega(E))\) and \((A', E', \Omega(E'))\), where \(E\) and \(E'\) are constants.
✦ Total energy (isolated)
✦
\(E^*\) \(=\) \(E + E'\)
✦ Total number of microstates (isolated)
✦
\(\Omega^*(E^*)\) \(=\) \(\Omega(E) \cdot \Omega'(E')\)
When the isolating constraint is removed — allowing energy exchange between \(A\) and \(A'\) — the individual energies \(E\) and \(E'\) are no longer constant, but the total energy remains fixed.
✦ Total energy (in contact)
✦
\(E^*\) \(=\) \(E + E'\)
✦ Energy of \(A'\) in terms of total
✦
\(E'\) \(=\) \(E^* - E\)
✦ Total microstates as a function of \(E\)
✦
\(\Omega^*(E)\) \(=\) \(\Omega(E) \cdot \Omega'(E^* - E)\)
✦ Sum over all possible energy partitions
✦
\(\Omega^*_f\) \(=\) \(\displaystyle\sum_E \Omega(E) \, \Omega'(E^* - E)\)
✦ Irreversible increase in microstates
✦
\(\Omega^*_f \ggg \Omega^*_i\)
✦ Corresponding entropy increase
✦
\(S^*_f \ggg S^*_i\)

Thermal Interaction

External parameters are held constant, so the energy levels of each system remain unchanged. Only heat is exchanged.
✦ Conservation of average energy
✦
\(\overline{E_i} + \overline{E'_i}\) \(=\) \(\overline{E_f} + \overline{E'_f}\)
✦ Change in average energy
✦
\(\overline{\Delta E_i} + \overline{\Delta E'_i}\) \(=\) \(0\)
✦ Heat exchange
✦
\(\overline{\Delta E} = Q,\quad \overline{\Delta E'} = Q'\)
✦ Total heat conservation
✦
\(Q + Q' = 0\)
✦ Heat lost equals heat gained
✦
\(Q = -Q'\)

Adiabatic Interaction

Systems \(A\) and \(A'\) are separated by an insulating material or placed far apart so that no heat flows (\(Q = 0\)). However, external parameters (e.g., volume, piston position) may change, allowing macroscopic work to be done.
✦ No heat exchange
✦
\(Q = 0\)
  • \(A\) and \(A'\) change to \(B\) and \(B'\).
  • Change in volume.
  • Moving a piston.
✦ Energy change equals work done
✦
\(\Delta E = W\)
where
\(W\) - macroscopic work done
✦ Conservation of total energy change
✦
\(\overline{\Delta E_i} + \overline{\Delta E'_i} = 0\)
✦ Work conservation
✦
\(W + W' = 0\)
6.2

Combined Thermal and Adiabatic Interaction

✦ First Law of Thermodynamics (system \(A\))
✦
\(\overline{\Delta E} = Q + W\)
✦ First Law of Thermodynamics (system \(A'\))
✦
\(\overline{\Delta E'} = Q' + W'\)
✦ Isolated total system
✦
\(\overline{\Delta E_i} + \overline{\Delta E'_i} = 0\)
