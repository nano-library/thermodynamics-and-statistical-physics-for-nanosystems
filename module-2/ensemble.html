✨ Consider, at a single instant of time, a large number of systems which are characterized by the same macrostate as the original system but are, naturally enough, in all sorts of possible microstates. Then, under ordinary circumstances, we may expect that the average behavior of any system in this collection, which we call an ensemble, would be identical to the time-averaged behavior of the given system.

Microcanonical Ensemble

An isolated system with fixed energy \(E\), fixed volume \(V\), and fixed number of particles \(N\); all accessible microstates are equally probable.

Canonical Ensemble

A system in thermal contact with a heat reservoir at temperature \(T\), allowing energy exchange but with fixed \(V\) and \(N\); the probability of a microstate is proportional to the Boltzmann factor \(e^{-\beta E_r}\).

Grand Canonical Ensemble

An open system in contact with both a heat and particle reservoir, allowing exchange of energy and particles; the probability of a state with energy \(E_r\) and particle number \(N_r\) is proportional to \(e^{-\beta (E_r - \mu N_r)}\).
